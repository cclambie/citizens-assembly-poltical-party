# Foundational Principles Statement
## Citizens Assembly/ Democratic Participation Party (Working Draft v1)

### Preamble

We believe democracy functions best when political power is exercised by informed citizens rather than concentrated interests, permanent political classes, or entrenched institutions.

We recognise that representative democracy remains necessary in a modern society, but that representation must be guided by structured public deliberation, civic participation, transparency, and accountability.

Our purpose is to create a democratic system in which elected representatives serve primarily as facilitators of citizen decision-making, ensuring that government remains accountable to the people it serves.

We affirm that political authority derives from the community, and that democratic participation should be accessible to all people regardless of wealth, status, background, education, or political affiliation.

The Party exists to strengthen democratic legitimacy, improve public trust, and build a political culture based on participation, evidence, deliberation, and civic responsibility.

## Principle 1: Popular Sovereignty

Ultimate democratic legitimacy rests with the people.

Government exists to serve the community and derives its authority from the informed participation of citizens.

The Party recognises that democratic participation is a civic right and a civic responsibility.

## Principle 2: Deliberative Democracy

Major public decisions should be informed by structured deliberative democratic processes.

Citizens' Assemblies, Citizens' Juries, Community Panels, and other deliberative mechanisms shall form the primary method through which the Party develops policy positions and determines responses to major public issues.

The Party commits to building permanent democratic infrastructure that enables continuous public participation in decision-making.

## Principle 3: Representatives as Civic Facilitators

Elected representatives are custodians of democratic participation rather than holders of personal political authority.

The primary responsibilities of elected representatives are:

- Facilitating public participation.
- Convening deliberative processes.
- Communicating community views to government institutions.
- Explaining governmental constraints and opportunities to citizens.
- Implementing Assembly recommendations to the greatest extent possible within their lawful powers.

Representatives shall swear an oath committing them to these obligations.

## Principle 4: Structured Democratic Authority

Where conflicting recommendations arise, decisions shall be resolved through a defined hierarchy of deliberative authority.

The hierarchy shall be:

1. Constitution and democratic safeguards.
2. National Citizens' Assemblies.
3. State or Territory Citizens' Assemblies.
4. Regional Citizens' Assemblies.
5. Electorate Citizens' Assemblies.
6. Local participatory forums.

Where lower-level assemblies produce conflicting recommendations, matters may be referred to higher-level assemblies for resolution.

Higher-level assemblies shall be larger, more representative, better resourced, and supported by expanded evidence-gathering processes.

## Principle 5: Civic Equality

Every citizen shall have an equal opportunity to participate in democratic processes.

The Party rejects systems in which wealth, influence, connections, or organisational power determine political outcomes.

Democratic opportunity must not be dependent upon financial capacity.

Participation systems shall be designed to ensure inclusion of people from all backgrounds and circumstances.

## Principle 6: Sortition and Representative Assemblies

The Party supports the use of random selection (sortition) as a democratic tool.

Citizens' Assemblies shall be selected using transparent and independently audited methods designed to achieve demographic representativeness.

Assembly design shall draw upon established best practice, including lessons from Australian, Irish, and international deliberative democracy processes.

Measures shall be adopted to prevent manipulation, capture, coercion, or undue influence.

## Principle 7: Evidence-Based Deliberation

All deliberative processes must be supported by balanced, transparent, and independently verified information.

Participants shall have access to:

- Expert evidence.
- Stakeholder perspectives.
- Community experiences.
- Independent research.
- Competing viewpoints.

The integrity of information provided to participants is essential to democratic legitimacy.

## Principle 8: Civic Participation as Public Service

Participation in democratic deliberation is a valuable contribution to society.

Citizens selected for assemblies shall receive fair compensation for their time, travel, care responsibilities, and other participation costs.

No citizen should suffer financial disadvantage as a result of democratic participation.

Special measures shall ensure participation remains accessible to:

- Workers.
- Small business operators.
- Carers.
- People with disabilities.
- Regional and rural communities.
- Unemployed citizens.
- Young people.

## Principle 9: Democratic Infrastructure

Permanent democratic participation requires permanent democratic infrastructure.

The Party supports the establishment of protected public funding for deliberative democratic institutions.

Funding should be secure, transparent, independently administered, and protected from political interference.

Democratic participation infrastructure should be treated as an essential institution of government, similar in importance to courts, elections, and public accountability bodies.

## Principle 10: Integrity and Anti-Corruption

All Party structures, representatives, assemblies, and institutions shall operate under rigorous integrity standards.

Independent auditing, disclosure requirements, complaint mechanisms, and corruption safeguards shall apply throughout the organisation.

No individual, faction, donor, officeholder, or institution shall be permitted to exercise unchecked authority.

Power must always remain subject to scrutiny.

## Principle 11: Rotation and Prevention of Power Concentration

Long-term concentration of authority undermines democratic legitimacy.

Leadership, facilitation, oversight, and governance roles shall be subject to rotation, term limits, and regular review.

No office within the Party shall be treated as permanent.

The Party rejects the creation of a professional political class.

## Principle 12: Democratic Accountability

Representatives remain accountable to the communities they serve.

A transparent complaints process shall allow citizens to raise concerns regarding breaches of democratic obligations.

Serious breaches of constitutional obligations may trigger investigation, public review, recall processes, or replacement mechanisms.

The community must retain meaningful power to remove representatives who fail to uphold their responsibilities.

## Principle 13: Emergency Governance

Emergency powers may only be exercised when immediate action is necessary to protect life, safety, public order, or essential democratic institutions.

Emergency powers shall:

- Be narrowly defined.
- Be publicly justified.
- Be independently reviewed.
- Automatically expire after three months unless renewed.

Any extension shall require a supermajority approval of Parliament of not less than 80%.

All emergency decisions shall be subject to post-event public review.

## Principle 14: Internal Democratic Governance

The Party itself shall be governed according to the same democratic principles it seeks to apply to society.

Party governance shall include:

- Elected members.
- Sortition-based citizen participation.
- Independent oversight structures.

No faction, executive body, or elected representative shall possess unilateral authority over Party direction.

## Principle 15: Constitutional Protection

This Constitution exists to protect democratic participation from future capture.

Constitutional amendments shall require:

- Broad member support.
- Independent review.
- Public consultation.
- Approval through multiple democratic channels.

The Constitution shall not be altered lightly.

## Principle 16: Continuous Democratic Improvement

No democratic system is perfect.

The Party commits to ongoing evaluation, experimentation, learning, and reform.

Deliberative processes, participation mechanisms, integrity systems, and governance structures shall be regularly reviewed and improved using evidence and public feedback.

### Foundational Test

Every decision, policy, appointment, process, institution, and reform supported by the Party must satisfy the following test:

"Does this strengthen democratic participation, increase civic agency, improve accountability, reduce the concentration of power, and ensure that political influence is determined by citizens rather than wealth, status, or privilege?"

If the answer is no, the proposal should not proceed.

### Foundational Motto

"Nothing about the people, without the people."
